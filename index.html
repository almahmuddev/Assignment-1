Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi economist and social entrepreneur, best known for founding the Grameen Bank and pioneering microcredit. He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for his efforts to alleviate poverty through microfinance, providing small loans to the impoverished without collateral. Yunus is a proponent of social business, focusing on solving social problems through sustainable business models. His work has inspired global microfinance initiatives.
